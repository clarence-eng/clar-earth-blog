silt exhales softly beneath boot soles
dawn's shimmer caught in ripples of sand, waves lace the shore with shifting lines.

seagrass reveals its green alphabet
a Fern, a Needle, a Spoon; blades sway – a living script to those who pause.

sea cucumbers shift beneath the sand
bumpy and soft like overripe fruit, breathing slowly with each returning wave.

hermit crabs scuttle sideways
tucked tight in borrowed spiral shells, legs scribble paths along the shore.

moon snails glide like secret engineers
laying sand collars – ghostly, precise, perfect rings pressed into shifting ground.

anemones bloom in tidepools
soft-tentacled, bathed in sunlight, they wave – tiny worlds curled open like mouths.

sand dollars, half-buried in silence
rest like coins cast for luck, fivefold stars veiled by tidal glaze.

sea squirts anchor to sponge and stone
filtering brine with steady rhythm – sentries that never speak, yet clean.

children dash, shrieking with delight
"squishy!" – "tiny legs!" – "look, a circle!" salt and laughter on every fingertip.

the shore becomes a shared classroom
where wonder loops from hand to hand, older hearts learning to see again.

each life here holds its quiet claim
not loud, not large, but sacred in a threshold between worlds.

conservation begins as stillness
but must rise – loud, loving, fierce – from the hands that hold, to those that heal.

a promise to protect what cannot speak
