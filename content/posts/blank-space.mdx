You called me "Dear"
as you filled me in all year.
Months and days I gave
head to re-live
memories as I ride
waves of emotion all night.

You'd reflect
in tales set erect.
I was your debauchee,
kissed by ecstasy
on my margins,
and the touch of your skin.

You inked profanities
as we shared your wildest fantasies.
Fingers clasped round my spine –
inside my sheets our lines entwine.
Then we'd climax
as you halt your pen, nib on the edge of precipice.

[right]
I waited in petulance,
for days turned to months to years.
Seems you pulled out for the last time,
and left me closed –
to wallow, age and yellow.

[right]
I watch with jaundice
as your fingers flutter
across her keys.
Click-click in staccato,
the way you penned me too.

[right]
I thought I brightened
your world.
But it pales to her
phoney incandescence,
consuming you through the night.

[right]
I feel empty now.
You just left me –
dirtied and creamed
in tears and coffee stains,
my pages a mere

[right]
space
