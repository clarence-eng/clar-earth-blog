Missing you comes in waves –
		the weight of your ghost crashes
				against my break wall, trembling beneath the pull of tides.
It yields to torrential yearning, swelling in pressure –
				cracking like skin in winter,
						splintering in the cold and ache,
								flaking to sand, drawn down where light won't go.
Clear sky bleeds to Stygian murk.
		The horizon melts,
				filling my chest with bitter spray and water I cannot hold.
Numb from the brine, my head a ringing shell –
				a din of wailing waves, rising
						in crescendo, deafening, breaking apart
								as everything crashes and folds into the dark.
I scream into a sea of silence.
		No one hears.
				And still –
						I am drowning.
