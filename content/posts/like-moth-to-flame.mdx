You shimmered soft, as if the light loved two,
A lure so gentle I mistook for fate.
I named your glow a bond I thought was true,
Too blind to sense the fire that hid in wait.
You let me hover, drawn by heat unknown,
Each orbit closer, dancing toward the flame.
No word was said, yet still you stood full blown,
A silent yes you never dared to name.
When I was burned, you only turned away,
As though my fall were dust upon the air.
You drew new moths with just your golden sway,
Their wings still whole, their hearts not yet aware.
You sparked the blaze, yet never took the blame.
I loved the flame – what folly, what a shame.
