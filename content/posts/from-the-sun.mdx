You're always late – a comic sight,
shoes half-laced, with bursts of laughter.
You chase me at the edge of light,
footsteps rush, then break and scatter.

You sat with ducks in morning gleam,
thoughts alight, and voices sincere.
The sky bent low to hold your dreams,
and I stayed near – just watching here.

I watched you pack with fading light,
low charge, no sleep, the road unclear.
Still you held fast, your hearts burnt bright,
while I pulled back and kept you near.

I hope you find your patch of peace –
a home by waves or forest floor.
I'll cross the skies without cease,
to warm your days a little more.

I rise for many, but I wait for you.
You call out "Uiii" and I shine through.
It's in your spark – I see it too.
